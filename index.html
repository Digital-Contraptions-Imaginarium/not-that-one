"Not That One!" app

 

... because you don't need
to know everything about food
to shop responsibly!

 

Shop lightheartedly *and* shop better

  • You don't need (and can't) know everything about food...
  • ... but packaged food often offers 'tells' that suggest that another equivalent product on the same shelf is likely (or surely) better.
  • So, why not let an app help you spot those tells?

Facts, opinions, preferences

The tells can be based on:
  • Fact, e.g. the maker is known for tax avoidance, or for having been affected by the horse meat scandal in 2013.
  • Opinion, e.g. some say drinking homogenised milk can increase risk of heart disease, or that we should better avoid eating food containing titanium dioxide.
  • Preference, e.g. one may believe that organic food is better for her and her family... or that, at least, that organic farming is more ethical and sustainable.

How does it work?

  • Not That One! is (will be) a free mobile app that quickly checks a product you are keen to buy against a database of "tells" created by yourself, friends or people and associations you trust.
  • Just point your mobile phone camera at the barcode of the product and see what "tells" Not That One! could find for you.
  • When you shop online, use participating websites only. You will see the same graphics alongside the product descriptions.

What if I don't agree with the app?

  • Well, you just need to define your own rules. The rules are saved in the form of a GitHub repository such as this.
  • You can "clone" (fork) the original rules and make a set of your own.
  • Moreover, you can contribute back and make the original better!
